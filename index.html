Welcome To Ondo State

The Sunshine State

Map of Ondo State

Ondo State is a state in southwestern Nigeria. It was created on February 3, 1976 from the former Western State. Ondo State borders Ekiti State to the north, Kogi State to the northeast, Edo State to the east, Delta State to the southeast, Ogun State to the southwest, Osun State to the northwest, and the Atlantic Ocean to the south. The state's capital is Akure, the former capital of the ancient Akure Kingdom. Ondo State includes mangrove-swamp forest near the Bights of Benin.

Nicknamed the "Sunshine State", Ondo State is the 19th most populated state in the country, and the 25th-largest state by landmass. The state is predominantly occupied by Yoruba speaking people. The state economy is dominated by the petroleum industry. Cocoa production, asphalt mining, and activities related to the state's extensive coastline also are part of the economy. It is the home to the Idanre inselberg hills, playing host to the highest geographical point in the western half of Nigeria at higher than 1,000 Meters in elevation.

Places Of Attractions

Araromi Beach

Araromi beach located in Ilaje local government area of Ondo state, is regarded as the largest coastline in West Africa. And am sure you are going to enjoy a visit.

Cave of Ashes

This was discovered in 1922 a tourist attraction, It was discovered during a hunting expedition. It is notable as one of the key sources to the information about the history of West Africa. The cave played a significant role in the archaeological history of West Africa. Its Located in Isharun, Ile-Oworo, in Ifedore Local Government of Ondo State.

Ebomi Lake

The Ebomi Lake Tourist Centre is located in Ipesi, in Akoko South-East. and it is not far from Akure. You can come here and glean a thing or two from the culture of the people. A lot of people are always coming here for relaxation and to explore nature.

Idanre Hills

The Idanre Hill, or Oke Idanre is located in Idanre town in Ondo State of southwestern Nigeria. The Idanre hill is well-known for its landscape.Its wide variety on cultural sites as 'Owa's Palace', shrines, The Old Court, Belfry, Agbooogun footprint, thunder water (Omi Apaara) and burial grounds have since brought the location fame and the nation's nomination for the UNESCO World Heritage Site shortlist. It resides 3000 ft (914.4meters) above sea level and houses a unique ecosystem upon which the cultural landscape has Integrated.

Igbo Olodumare

It is famed to be home to demons, spirits and other supernatural beings. Located in Oke Igbo town of Ile-Oluj/Oke-Igbo LGA in Ondo state, adventurers, tourists are welcome to confirm this myth, after all, seeing is believing. It is a cultural heritage site. It is so famous that a book was published for it.

Igbokoda Waterfront

The Igbokoda waterfront is the longest territorial water in Nigeria and has fishing terminal. The vast expanse of water makes for an exciting fishing expedition and the scenery is beautiful.It is the longest territorial water in Nigeria and has fishing terminal. The waterfront also offers an excellent location for boating, sport fishing, picnic, boat regatta, diving and many more. It's Location is in Igbokoda, Ondo State. Which is is located about 142km from Akure.

Smoking Hill Golf Course

This golf course with about 140 acres of hilly mounds covered with smokey fog is one lovely sight to behold. Smoking Hills Golf Course is located on the outskirts of Ilara Mokin in Ifedore Local Government Area of Ondo State.
